## SCRIPT STRUCTURE

Every Short follows the narrative writing model of its content pillar.
The total narration must be **130–180 words** (45–60 seconds at natural
speaking pace). The structure mirrors the VidIQ writing models:
**Intro & Hook → Section(s) → CTA & Conclusion**.

> **CRITICAL:** This is NOT a listicle format. The script is a
> **flowing narrative** written in second person, as if a mentor is
> speaking directly to the viewer. Study the writing model file for
> the pillar before writing.

---

### [INTRO & HOOK — 0 to 8 seconds] (40–55 words)

The hook is NOT a single statement. It's a **micro-narrative of
transformation** that pulls the viewer into a story within the first
3 seconds.

**Narrative arc (follow this pattern):**

1. **Before state** (pain/weakness):
   "He used to be [relatable suffering], constantly [vivid detail]."
2. **After state** (transformation):
   "Now, he [aspirational result] with [concrete detail]."
3. **Curiosity gap**:
   "What's the difference?" / "What changed?" / "How?"
4. **Dismiss the obvious**:
   "It's not magic." / "It's not talent." / "It's not luck."
5. **Reveal the mechanism**:
   "It's the [N] [topic] that [power statement]."

Rules:
- The before/after contrast must be emotionally vivid — not abstract
- The "before" must be something the viewer secretly fears they are
- The "after" must be what they desperately want to become
- The curiosity question creates a micro-pause that locks attention
- The reveal connects directly to the title's promise
- No greetings, no preambles, no "hey guys" — start mid-story

**Hook variation by pillar (adapt the before/after to match tone):**

| Pillar | "Before" tone | "After" tone |
|---|---|---|
| Stoic Lessons | Emotional victim | Calm, unshakable |
| Dark Psychology | Naive, manipulated | Aware, in control |
| Mind Control | Overthinking, anxious | Disciplined, focused |
| Human Nature | Trusting, blind | Eyes open, strategic |
| Laws of Power | Powerless, ignored | Commanding, respected |

> `VISUAL: [First frame shows a lone figure or marble bust in shadow —
> movement required (slow zoom or pan). On the "Now" transition, shift
> to a brighter side-lit angle or new scene. Key phrase from the reveal
> displayed in ember gold, center screen.]`

---

### [SECTION(S) — 8 to 45 seconds] (70–100 words)

The body is a **conversational, flowing narrative** — NOT a numbered
bullet list. The content weaves insights together in connected
paragraphs that feel like a mentor talking directly to the viewer.

**Narrative pattern (follow this flow):**

1. **Clarification opener** — Set expectations and reframe:
   "And let's be clear: this isn't about [common misconception].
   It's about [powerful reframe]."

2. **Promise + technique** — Pull the viewer in with "you":
   "You'll learn how to [specific skill], finally understanding
   that [deeper truth beneath the surface]."

3. **Embedded rhetorical question** — Create internal dialogue:
   Plant a question the viewer asks themselves. This makes them
   feel the content is personal, not generic.
   "Will this crisis even matter in a year?"

4. **Insight escalation** — Each sentence builds on the previous:
   Add a new angle, a deeper layer, or a harder truth. Never
   repeat the same intensity twice.

5. **Power closer** — End with a strong reframe:
   "This is how you stop being [weakness] and start [strength]."
   The final sentence must redefine the viewer's relationship
   to the topic.

**Writing principles (apply to every section):**

1. **Conversational flow:** The script must sound like a person
   talking, not a list being read. Use transitions: "And let's be
   clear...", "You'll also learn...", "This is how you..."

2. **Second person immersion:** Use "you" and "your" constantly.
   The viewer must feel this is about THEM, not abstract philosophy.
   "You'll learn how to create that sacred pause between what
   happens and how you react."

3. **Concrete over abstract:** Every concept must be grounded in
   something the viewer can picture. "Control your emotions" becomes
   "that sacred pause between what happens and how you react."

4. **Rhetorical questions as engagement hooks:** Plant questions
   that make the viewer answer internally. This creates psychological
   investment and increases watch time.

5. **No filler phrases:** Ban: "you see", "the thing is", "it's
   important to note", "let me tell you", "basically", "actually",
   "what I mean is". Every word must carry weight.

6. **Power language:** Use words that hit: silence, power, control,
   weak, dangerous, untouchable, unshakable, destroy, discipline,
   respect, invisible, ruthless, ancient, forbidden, fortress,
   puppet, commanding, citadel.

7. **Rhythm:** Mix short punchy sentences (3–6 words) with longer
   flowing ones (15–25 words). The text should pulse when read aloud.

> `VISUAL: [Change visuals every 3–5 seconds of narration. Cycle
> through: statue close-up → urban night scene → silhouette → Greek
> ruins → chess piece → rain on glass. Text overlays highlight key
> phrases in ember gold. Apply Permanent Visual Layer.]`

---

### [CTA & CONCLUSION — 45 to 60 seconds] (20–35 words)

The closer does three things: (1) issues a direct challenge to the
viewer, (2) drives a subscribe/follow action tied to content value,
and (3) sparks a comment with a specific question.

**Pattern:**

1. **Challenge question** (before/after framing):
   "So, are you ready to stop [pain] and start [transformation]?"
2. **Value-tied follow**:
   "If the answer is yes, subscribe for more [content promise]."
3. **Comment trigger** (specific to the body content):
   "Let's start the challenge now: [concrete question]? Comment below."

**CTA templates (rotate — never repeat for consecutive Shorts):**

- "So, are you ready to stop [pain] and start [transformation]?
  Subscribe for more stoic wisdom. What's the one habit you'll change
  first? Comment below."
- "If this changed how you see [topic], subscribe for daily truths
  most people avoid. Which part hit hardest? Drop it below."
- "The stoics built their fortress one habit at a time. Are you building
  yours? Subscribe and comment the habit you're starting today."
- "This is how you [transformation]. If you're ready, subscribe.
  What's your biggest weakness right now? Be honest below."
- "Remember this the next time [situation from the body]. Subscribe
  for more. What's one thing you'll stop doing after this?"

Rules:
- Always include a subscribe/follow prompt tied to content value —
  never a naked "like and subscribe"
- The comment trigger must reference specific content from the body
- The challenge must use before/after framing (stop X, start Y)
- Maximum 3 sentences
- The viewer should still be thinking about it 10 seconds after swiping

> `VISUAL: [Final frame holds for 3–4 seconds. Challenge question text
> in bone white, center screen. Dark background. Subtle fade or hold.
> Subscribe animation optional.]`
